# Email Sequence Plan

## Source Status

The client-specific offer, audience, tone, compliance requirements, and sales cycle were not provided. This plan defines sequence structure and required decision points only. It does not include final client-facing copy.

## Email Strategy

Use email sequences to support timely follow-up without removing human ownership from the sales process. Monday.com can trigger reminders, create tasks, and in some cases send emails, but final send method should be confirmed based on deliverability, compliance, and the client's existing email tools.

## Recommended Sequence Types

| Sequence | Audience | Goal | Trigger | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| New inquiry response | New inbound leads | Acknowledge inquiry and prompt next step. | Lead created. | Sales owner |
| Discovery scheduling | Qualified leads | Book discovery or consultation. | Lead marked qualified. | Sales owner |
| Post-discovery follow-up | Prospects after meeting | Recap discussion and confirm next action. | Discovery completed. | Sales owner |
| Proposal follow-up | Open deals with proposal sent | Drive decision and answer objections. | Proposal / quote sent. | Sales owner |
| Stalled deal nurture | Active deals with no recent activity | Re-engage without over-automation. | No activity for defined period. | Sales owner |
| Closed-lost nurture | Lost or not-ready opportunities | Keep future opportunity alive. | Deal marked closed-lost or nurture. | Sales / marketing |
| Closed-won handoff | New customers | Confirm next steps and introduce delivery. | Deal marked closed-won. | Sales and delivery |

## Example Sequence Structure

### New Inquiry Response

| Step | Timing | Purpose | Automation Role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Immediate or same business day | Acknowledge inquiry and set expectation. | Trigger email or create task. |
| 2 | 1 business day later | Follow up if no response. | Reminder or email. |
| 3 | 3 business days later | Final attempt before nurture. | Reminder or email. |

### Proposal Follow-Up

| Step | Timing | Purpose | Automation Role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Same day proposal sent | Confirm proposal delivery. | Create task or send email. |
| 2 | 2-3 business days later | Ask for questions and decision timing. | Reminder or email. |
| 3 | 5-7 business days later | Reconfirm fit and next step. | Reminder or email. |
| 4 | Defined by client | Move to nurture or manager review. | Update task or notify owner. |

## Personalization Fields To Confirm

- Contact first name.
- Company name.
- Sales owner name.
- Sales owner email and phone.
- Service, package, or offer of interest.
- Meeting date.
- Proposal link.
- Next step or scheduling link.

## Compliance And Deliverability Questions

- Will emails be sent from individual inboxes, Monday.com, or a marketing automation tool?
- Does the client have consent to email imported contacts?
- Does the sequence need unsubscribe language?
- Are there industry-specific communication requirements?
- Should any emails require manual approval before sending?

## Recommended Approach

Start with internal task-based reminders for follow-up. Move to automated external emails only after the client approves copy, sending method, suppression rules, and compliance requirements.
