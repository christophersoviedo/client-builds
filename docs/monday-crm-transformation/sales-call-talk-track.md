# Sales Call Talk Track

## Positioning

The goal is to turn Monday.com into a usable CRM operating system, not just a board where sales tasks are stored. The system should make the sales process visible, reduce missed follow-ups, and give leadership reliable pipeline reporting.

## Opening

Thanks for taking the time to walk through the CRM project. The main thing I want to understand is how your sales process actually works today, where information gets lost, and what Monday.com needs to show the team every day.

I will not assume your stages, metrics, or integrations until we confirm them. The build should match the way your team sells, while tightening the structure enough to make reporting and automation reliable.

## Discovery Flow

### Current State

- How are leads captured today?
- Where do contacts, companies, and deals currently live?
- What tools does the team use every day?
- What parts of the sales process are manual or inconsistent?
- Where do follow-ups get missed?

### Sales Workflow

- What happens after a new inquiry comes in?
- Who qualifies the lead?
- When does a lead become a deal?
- What stages does a deal move through?
- What information is required before a deal can be marked closed-won?
- What happens after the sale is closed?

### Reporting

- What does leadership need to see weekly?
- What does a sales manager need to manage daily?
- What does a rep need to prioritize each morning?
- Do you track deal value, forecast close date, source, and loss reason today?

### Automation

- What reminders or notifications would immediately save time?
- What should happen when a lead is assigned?
- What should happen when a proposal is sent?
- What should happen when a deal is won?
- What should happen when a deal is lost?

### Integrations

- Which systems need to send data into Monday.com?
- Which systems need data from Monday.com?
- Are there any tools that must remain the source of truth?
- Who can provide access to the tools we need to connect?

## Recommended Explanation

I would typically structure this as a connected CRM: leads, contacts, companies, deals, activities, and onboarding handoff. That keeps each board focused and lets dashboards report accurately. If everything lives on one board, it can look simple at first but usually becomes harder to manage as reporting and automation needs grow.

The first priority is the data model. Once the boards and fields are right, automations and dashboards become much easier to trust.

## Handling Common Concerns

### "Can this all be automated?"

Some parts can and should be automated, especially assignment, reminders, notifications, and handoffs. I would be careful about automating anything that changes deal status or sends external emails until the workflow and copy are approved.

### "Can we import our existing data?"

Usually yes, but the quality of the import depends on cleanup. We should map the fields, remove duplicates where possible, and run a test import before bringing everything over.

### "Can Monday.com replace our CRM?"

It can for many teams, but the answer depends on your sales complexity, integrations, reporting needs, and data volume. Discovery should confirm whether Monday.com should replace the existing CRM or connect with it.

### "How long will this take?"

Without confirmed scope, a practical estimate is phased: discovery first, then board build, automations, dashboards, integrations, migration, and training. Timeline depends heavily on data cleanup and integration access.

## Close

The next step is to confirm the workflow, required data, reporting priorities, and integration list. From there, we can finalize the build plan and avoid designing around assumptions.
